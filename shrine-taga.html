Enshrined Deities
Izanagi no Mikoto & Izanami no Mikoto
伊邪那岐命・伊邪那美命 — The Divine Couple Who Created Japan
Izanagi and Izanami are the primordial creator deities of Japanese mythology. They churned the ocean with a jeweled spear and formed the Japanese islands; they gave birth to the myriad gods, including Amaterasu Omikami, the Sun Goddess enshrined at Ise Jingu. Taga Taisha is where these parent gods retired after completing their divine work of creation — making this shrine the spiritual origin point of all Japanese divinity.

The Essence · 核心価値 核心的価値 — なぜここが特別なのか 核心价值 · 为何与众不同
由緒 · Why It Matters
The home of the gods who created Japan itself.
The ancient chronicle Kojiki (712 AD) records that after creating the world, Izanagi no Mikoto dwelt at Taga in Omi Province. With over 1,300 years of history, Taga Taisha is revered as the ultimate shrine for longevity and en-musubi (binding of fate) — the parent deities who gave birth to all of creation are naturally the supreme authority on life and love.
深読み · The Deeper Story
In 1588, the great warlord Toyotomi Hideyoshi donated a magnificent stone bridge and garden to Taga Taisha, praying for the recovery of his ailing mother, Omandokoro. She miraculously recovered and lived several more years. This story cemented Taga Taisha's reputation as the supreme shrine for longevity. The saying “O-Ise mairi ni O-Taga e yokare” (“After visiting Ise, do visit Taga”) became a national proverb — linking the daughter shrine (Ise) to its spiritual parents (Taga).

Deep Insight · 深度探索 リピーターのこだわり — 知る人ぞ知る 深度探索 · 行家的坚持
UNIQUE
太閥橋 — Taiko-bashi: Hideyoshi's Stone Bridge
The beautiful arched stone bridge at the entrance was donated by Toyotomi Hideyoshi in 1588, in gratitude for his mother's miraculous recovery. Crossing this steep, elegant bridge is said to grant longevity. Its graceful curve reflected in the water below is the iconic image of Taga Taisha.
SECRET
奥書院庭園 — Okusho-in: A Hidden Masterpiece
Behind the main hall, the Okusho-in Garden is a nationally designated Place of Scenic Beauty (meisho). Created in the Azuchi-Momoyama period (late 16th century), this contemplative pond garden was also a gift from Hideyoshi. Few visitors venture here — making it one of Shiga's best-kept secrets. Admission ¥300.
UNIQUE
しゃもじ — The Rice Paddle Tradition
Taga Taisha's signature lucky charm is the shamoji (rice paddle). Write your wish on a wooden shamoji and offer it at the shrine. This unique custom symbolizes the nourishment and sustenance of life — the rice paddle that feeds all. You will see thousands of shamoji hanging in the shrine precincts.
BEST TIME
春の枝垂桜 — Weeping Cherry Blossoms in Spring
Late March to early April transforms Taga Taisha into a cascade of pink. The massive weeping cherry trees (shidarezakura) along the approach and around the Taiko-bashi bridge create one of Shiga's most breathtaking spring scenes. The combination of the arched bridge and falling petals is unforgettable.
GOSHUIN
御朱印 — Ichinomiya Pilgrimage Stamp
Taga Taisha participates in the zenkoku ichinomiya goshuin collection. The powerful brushwork and distinctive seal mark it as an Omi Province ichinomiya stamp. Office open 8:30–16:30 · ¥500. Seasonal limited-edition goshuin are also available.

Food · 食
近江牛 Omi Beef
One of Japan's top three wagyu brands, Omi beef has been raised in Shiga for over 400 years. Try it at local restaurants near Hikone or along the shrine approach road.
🍡
Sweets · 甘味
糸切餅 Itokiri Mochi
Taga's signature sweet — soft rice cakes cut with thread, filled with sweet red bean paste. Sold at traditional shops along the shrine approach. A must-try pilgrimage treat.
🏯
Sightseeing · 観光
彦根城 Hikone Castle
One of only 12 original castles in Japan and a National Treasure. Just 15 min by train from Taga. Don't miss the iconic mascot Hikonyan and the stunning lake views.
🏨
Stay · 宿
Hikone Hotels & Ryokan
Stay in Hikone city for easy access to both Taga Taisha and Hikone Castle. Traditional ryokan with lake views and Omi beef dinners available near the castle.
🌊
Nature · 自然
琵琶湖 Lake Biwa
Japan's largest lake is just west of Taga. Cycling the lakeshore, boat cruises, and lakeside onsen make for a perfect complement to your shrine visit.

🏝️ Eight Great Islands Japan itself · Created by the Divine Couple
The ancient proverb “O-Ise mairi ni O-Taga e yokare” was once known throughout Japan: after completing the great pilgrimage to Ise Jingu, one must also visit Taga — because Ise enshrines Amaterasu, the daughter, while Taga enshrines Izanagi and Izanami, her parents who created the entire world. During the Edo period, millions of pilgrims traveled the Tokaido road, and Taga became an essential stop on the spiritual circuit. This parent-child bond between Ise and Taga remains one of the most profound connections in all of Shinto theology.
