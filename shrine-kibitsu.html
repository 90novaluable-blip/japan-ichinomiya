Enshrined Deity — The Real Momotaro
Okibitsuhiko no Mikoto
大吉備津彦命（おおきびつひこのみこと） — 桃太郎伝説の元祖
Okibitsuhiko no Mikoto is the legendary prince who subjugated the demon Ura (鬼) and brought peace to the Kibi region in ancient times. This historical figure became the inspiration for the beloved Momotaro (Peach Boy) folk tale known throughout Japan. The shrine stands at the very location of his legendary victory, making it the true origin point of one of Japan's most enduring legends. The Narukama divination ritual—a unique practice of interpreting the groans of a sacred rice cauldron—connects directly to this mythological triumph.

由緒 · Where Momotaro Was Born
The legendary shrine where the real demon-slayer defeated his adversary.
Founded in the legendary age, Kibitsu Shrine is venerated as the origin point of the Momotaro tale. Prince Okibitsuhiko subdued the demon Ura in a great battle, an event that crystallized into the beloved folktale taught to Japanese children for over a thousand years. The shrine's main hall, rebuilt in 1425 after centuries of worship, is Japan's finest example of Kibitsu-zukuri architecture—a style found nowhere else and designated a National Treasure.
奇跡の回廊 · The 360-Meter Corridor
Extending from the main worship hall is a remarkable 360-meter covered corridor (kairo) that winds through the shrine grounds—one of Japan's longest and most beautiful covered walkways. Roofed in dark wooden planks, the corridor creates an experience of sacred progression, gradually drawing worshippers deeper into the shrine's spiritual heart. Walking beneath this endless wooden ceiling, you feel time collapse—as if stepping between the modern world and the age of demons and heroes.

Deep Insight · 深度探索 リピーターのこだわり — 知る人ぞ知る 深度探索 · 行家的坚持
UNIQUE
吉備津造 — Kibitsu-zukuri Architectural Style
The main hall and worship hall (honden + haiden combined) represent a completely unique architectural style found only at this shrine. The structure, rebuilt in 1425 and designated a National Treasure, combines elements of shrine and temple architecture in a way that exists nowhere else in Japan. The main building is actually larger than the honden of the far more famous Izumo Taisha.
UNIQUE
鳴釜 — The Narukama Divination Ritual
One of Japan's most unusual divination practices: a priest places a rice cauldron (kama) on a large stone and interprets its groaning sounds to tell your fortune. The ancient cauldron literally "speaks" through vibrations, creating an uncanny, powerful divination experience. Available for visitors (fee required). This practice connects directly to the Momotaro legend and Okibitsuhiko's victory over the demon.
TIP
回廊散策 — The Sacred Corridor Walk
Don't rush through the 360-meter covered corridor. Walk slowly, observing how the light filters through the wooden roof, listening to the echo of footsteps. This corridor is one of Japan's most atmospheric spaces and creates a profound sense of being transported through time. Several smaller shrines and stone monuments line the route.
FESTIVAL
吉備津采女祭 Kibitsu Sayonume Matsuri — October
An ancient festival commemorating a tragic historical tale of a shrine maiden who drowned in the sacred pool. The festival includes processions, archery rituals, and a re-enactment of the legend. The atmosphere is haunting and deeply spiritual, quite different from cheerful modern festivals.
INFO
桃の木の祝福 — The Blessing of Peach Trees
Because of the Momotaro connection, peach (momo) imagery is everywhere in Okayama. The shrine itself has ancient peach trees on its grounds. Local tradition holds that peach blossoms represent renewal and the slaying of demons—appropriate symbols for a shrine dedicated to the hero who defeated evil.

Food · 食
Kibidango (Momotaro Dumplings)
Sweet red bean dumplings made famous by the Momotaro legend. Available throughout Okayama and Kibitsu area. The most iconic souvenir from the shrine, traditionally eaten before continuing your journey.
🍖
Local · 食
Okayama Ramen
Dark soy-based broth with thin noodles and a raw egg on top. The signature ramen style of Okayama City, hearty and distinctive. Several excellent shops near Kibitsu Station.
♨️
Stay · 宿
Okayama City Hotels
Excellent hotels and ryokan throughout Okayama City. The shrine is a day trip from Okayama or a base for exploring the entire region. Traditional inns near the shrine for an intimate experience.

History · 歴史
Okayama Korakuen Garden
One of Japan's three greatest landscape gardens, created in the 1700s. A 20-minute walk from Kibitsu Station via downtown Okayama. A masterpiece of garden design with sweeping views and perfect seasonal beauty.
🏛️
Charm · 町並
Kurashiki Bikan Quarter
A beautifully preserved historic merchant district about 30 minutes by train. Canal-side warehouses converted to museums, galleries, and shops. The aesthetic atmosphere complements the shrine visit perfectly.

The Momotaro folktale is one of Japan's most beloved children's stories, known throughout the world. Yet few realize its origin lies in a historical figure—Prince Okibitsuhiko, who defeated the demon Ura (representing indigenous resistance) and brought the Kibi region under imperial control. This shrine marks the exact location of that legendary victory. The story's elements—the peach birth, three loyal companions, the sharing of kibidango dumplings—all have roots in historical and spiritual practice. The shrine's magnificent main hall, rebuilt in 1425 in the unique Kibitsu-zukuri style, stands as a testament to centuries of reverence. Walking the 360-meter corridor feels like stepping between the world of history and the realm of folktale, where demons roamed and heroes triumphed.
