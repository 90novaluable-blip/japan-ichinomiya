Konohanasakuya-hime
木花之佐久夜毘売命 · 浅間大神

The radiant goddess of Mount Fuji and blooming flowers — her very name means 'She Who Makes the Blossoms Bloom.' She is said to have proven her purity by giving birth amid a raging fire lit by her jealous husband, emerging unscathed with her children. Today she watches over fertility, safe childbirth, and the volcanic fires of Japan's most sacred mountain.

✦ The Essence
Head shrine of over 1,300 Sengen shrines across Japan — and the spiritual guardian of Mount Fuji itself, designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
The shrine's origins trace back over 2,000 years. When Mount Fuji's eruptions threatened the land, Emperor Suinin ordered that the volcano's fierce spirit — Asama no Okami — be enshrined and appeased. The present magnificent hall was built by Tokugawa Ieyasu in 1604, just after his victory at the Battle of Sekigahara, as an offering of gratitude. Its unique 'Sengen-zukuri' two-story architectural style, designated a National Important Cultural Property, was created specifically for this shrine and found nowhere else in Japan.

✦ Getting There
  • 🚅From Tokyo: ~2 hrs — Tokaido Shinkansen to Shin-Fuji (Kodama, ~1 hr) → JR Minobu Line to Fujinomiya (~35 min)
  • 🚉Walk from Fujinomiya Station: ~15 min on foot, heading north. Very manageable.
  • 🚌Miya Buses run between Fujinomiya Station and the shrine 1–2 times per hour.
  • 🚗By car: Tomei Expressway → Fujinomiya IC, approx 20 min. Paid parking lots nearby.
  • 🕐Hours: Nov–Feb 6:00–19:00 · Mar & Oct 5:30–19:30 · Apr–Sep 5:00–20:00. Admission free.
  • ♿Mostly accessible grounds. The inner precinct and Wakutama Pond area are relatively flat.
✦ Insider's Guide
UNIQUE

Mount Fuji itself is the sacred object (shintai) of this shrine — not a statue or mirror, but the actual volcano. This makes it one of very few shrines in Japan where a living natural landmark is the deity's body.

TIP

Don't miss the Wakutama Pond (湧玉池) behind the main hall. Fed entirely by snowmelt from Mount Fuji, it's a designated Special Natural Monument. The water is crystal clear and icy cold — local climbers still purify themselves here before ascending the mountain.

SEASONAL

Late March to early April: 500 cherry trees explode into bloom along the Sakuranobaba parade path. On May 5, the famous yabusame (horseback archery) festival takes place along this same path — an unmissable spectacle.

HISTORY

The current main hall was personally commissioned by Tokugawa Ieyasu in 1604 as a votive offering after unifying Japan. The ornate 'Sengen-zukuri' style — with its distinctive tiered roof mimicking Mt. Fuji's silhouette — is a National Important Cultural Property.

TIP

For Mt. Fuji climbers: tradition s!ys you must pray here before ascending and again after you safely return. The shrine's inner sanctum sits at the very summit of Mt. Fuji too — the Okumiya (奥宮) — which you can visit at the top.

✦ Eat · Stay · Explore
🍽️ Must Eat
Fujinomiya Yakisoba
B-1 Grand Prix champion twice — chewy noodles with lard toppings, unique to this town. Try it at Okonomimura (3 min walk).
Omomiya Yokocho
Food street right next to the shrine with local specialties including masu sushi and Fuji trout.
🏨 Stay
Fujinomiya City Center Hotels
Several business hotels near Fujinomiya Station offer Mt. Fuji views. Best base for both shrine visits and mountain climbing.
Lake Tanuki Ryokan Area
For a scenic stay, Lake Tanuki (30 min by car) offers traditional ryokan with stunning double-diamond Fuji reflections at dawn.
🗺️ Explore More
Shiraito Falls
UNESCO World Heritage site (20 min by bus). Fuji's snowmelt cascades from a 150m-wide cliff edge in thousands of silken threads.
