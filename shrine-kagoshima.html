The Essence · 核心価値 核心的価値 — なぜここが特別なのか 核心价值 · 为何与众不同
由緒 · Where Heaven Met Earth
The legendary Takachiho Palace where the Imperial line was born in the Age of the Gods.
Kagoshima Jingu marks one of Japan's most mythologically significant sites. According to tradition, Hoori no Mikoto, the grandson of Amaterasu and ancestor of the first emperor, made this place his Takachiho Palace. Here, he married Toyotama-hime, the sea princess, and fathered the lineage that would rule Japan eternally. The shrine is Japan's sole Shikinai Taisha (officially sanctioned great shrine) in southern Kyushu, recognized in the Engishiki (10th century official shrine registry) as the preeminent shrine of Osumi Province.
伝説 · Ryugu Legend Connection
The myth of Hoori and the sea princess mirrors the timeless tale of the Dragon Palace (Ryugu) found throughout East Asian mythology. Toyotama-hime's father was a sea god, and their union created a bridge between heaven and ocean. Kirishima's location at the volcanic heart of southern Kyushu, near the sea, embodies this cosmic harmony. The shrine's landscape bridges myth and natural beauty.

Deep Insight · 深度探索 リピーターのこだわり — 知る人ぞ知る 深度探索 · 行家的坚持
UNIQUE
Engishiki Recognition — Only Southern Kyushu Taisha
In the Engishiki (10th century official registry of sacred shrines), Kagoshima Jingu is the only Shikinai Taisha listed for southern Kyushu. This status places it in the most elite category of Japanese shrines, signifying its importance as a conduit between the imperial court and southern regional power.
UNIQUE
Takachiho Palace Mythology — Seat of the Divine Ancestor
The shrine grounds are believed to be where Takachiho Palace once stood — the residence of Hoori no Mikoto during the Age of the Gods. This is not merely a temple built in honor of a deity, but the legendary actual dwelling place. Walking the shrine's paths, visitors trace the steps of Japan's imperial mythology.
INFO
Hayato People Connection — Indigenous Southern Warriors
Hayato were the indigenous warrior people of southern Kyushu. The shrine's location and mythology connect to the historical Hayato culture and their eventual integration into the emerging Japanese state. This shrine represents a bridge between imperial mythology and local southern heritage.
FESTIVAL
Hatsu-uma Matsuri — August Horse Festival
The main annual festival is held on August 15 by the lunar calendar. "Hatsu-uma" refers to the first horse day in the traditional calendar. The festival celebrates the divine ancestors and draws pilgrims from across Kyushu, featuring traditional performances and ceremonies honoring the Imperial lineage.
TIP
Ancient Camphor Trees — Living Connections to Antiquity
Like Oyamazumi Shrine, Kagoshima Jingu features ancient camphor trees that have witnessed centuries of worship. These venerable trees are living links to Japan's oldest spiritual practices. Visitors often touch or embrace the trees as a way of connecting to the deep past.

Food · 食
Kurobuta Pork
Black Berkshire pork raised in Kirishima's volcanic soil is an Osumi delicacy. Rich, flavorful meat served grilled, in hot pot, or as tonkatsu (breaded cutlet). Try it at local restaurants near Hayato Station.

Stay · 宿
Kirishima Hot Springs
Traditional ryokan nestled in volcanic foothills. Multiple onsen fed by Kirishima's natural hot springs. The perfect base for combining shrine pilgrimage with mountain relaxation.

Kirishima Mountain Park
Scenic hiking in volcanic terrain with crater lakes and mountain views. Multiple trails range from short walks to full-day treks. Connected to Kirishima Jingu (another major shrine).

Sakurajima Active Volcano
Visible from many points around Kagoshima, Japan's most active volcano can be reached by ferry from Kagoshima City. Watch steam and ash eruptions in real-time — 40 min from shrine by train.

Kagoshima Jingu represents one of Japan's most mythologically resonant sites — the legendary point where heaven descended to earth, where the divine ancestor married a sea princess, and where the Imperial lineage that would rule Japan for two millennia was born. Recognized in the Engishiki as the only Shikinai Taisha shrine in southern Kyushu, Kagoshima Jingu holds unique spiritual authority. The shrine's location in Kirishima, surrounded by volcanic mountains and connected geographically to the sea, reflects the cosmic harmony of its founding mythology. Unlike shrines built centuries after the events they commemorate, Kagoshima Jingu is believed to stand at the actual site of Takachiho Palace. For those seeking to understand Japan's deepest mythological foundations, this shrine in southern Kyushu offers an experience of encountering the very origins of an eternal imperial civilization.
