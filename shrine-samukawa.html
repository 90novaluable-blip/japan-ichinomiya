Enshrined Deity
Samukawa Daimyojin
寒川大明神 — Samukawahiko-no-mikoto & Samukawahime-no-mikoto
These two deities are unique to Samukawa Shrine — they are not enshrined anywhere else in Japan. Their divine power is Happo-yoke: protection against all calamities that may come from any of the eight directions. Warriors including Minamoto no Yoritomo, Takeda Shingen, and the Tokugawa clan all sought their blessing before major undertakings.

由緒 · Why It Matters
全国唯一の「八方除」の聖地。
The only shrine in all of Japan presiding over Happo-yoke — divine protection against every misfortune, from every direction, on every auspicious and inauspicious day. Founded over 1,600 years ago, it receives more formal prayers (kito) than any other shrine in Japan.
深読み · The Deeper Story
In Japanese cosmology, misfortune often arrives from unlucky directions — a concept called katatagae. Samukawa's deities neutralise all eight directions simultaneously. This is not merely about compass points — it encompasses time, fate, and the forces that surround human life. Even today, people come here before moving house, starting a business, or facing a major life change.

SECRET
神嶽山神苑 — The Hidden Inner Garden
Behind the main hall lies the Kantakeyama Shinen — a sacred forest garden once forbidden to all. Open only to those who have received a formal prayer (kito). Inside: ancient ponds, primeval cedars, and the divine spring that is considered the spiritual source of the entire shrine. Worth scheduling your visit around.
BEST TIME
早朝 8:00 — First Light & Birdsong
The shrine opens at 8:00 am. The first 30 minutes are extraordinary: no crowds, mist still low through the cedar trees, and only birdsong breaking the silence. On weekends this window closes fast.
GOSHUIN
御朱印 — Ichinomiya Pilgrimage Compatible
The shrine participates in the zenkoku ichinomiya goshuin collection. The bronze Kontenki (celestial sphere) design appears in the stamp — one of the most distinctive in the Kanto region. Office open 8:00–16:30 · ¥500.
ANNUAL
流鏑馬 Yabusame — September
Every September, the Takeda-ryū school of horseback archery performs yabusame on the shrine's sacred track. Mounted archers in full Kamakura-era costume shoot at targets at full gallop. One of the most dramatic Shinto rituals you can witness in Japan.
SYMBOL
渾天儀 — The Bronze Celestial Sphere
The striking Kontenki armillary sphere in front of the worship hall symbolises the harmony of heaven and earth — a uniquely scientific expression of the shrine's cosmic protective power. Excellent photograph opportunity.

In traditional Japanese belief, misfortune arrives from specific directions depending on the year, month, and day — a concept deeply embedded in divination and daily life planning. Happo-yoke (八方除) means neutralising all eight cardinal and intercardinal directions simultaneously. Samukawa Shrine is the only shrine in Japan with this specific divine authority. Moving to a new home, starting a company, or facing an unlucky year? This is where Japan's people come.
