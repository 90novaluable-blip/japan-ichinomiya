Enshrined Deities
The Three Pioneer Gods & Emperor Meiji
開拓三神 · 大国魂神 · 大那牟遅神 · 少彦名神 · 明治天皇
In 1869, Emperor Meiji chose three powerful deities to protect the opening of Hokkaido: the spirit of the land itself (Ōkunitama), the master of nation-building (Ōnamuji), and the god of medicine, sake, and crafts (Sukunahikona). Together, they are called the Three Pioneer Gods (開拓三神) — chosen specifically because their combined powers covered everything needed to settle a wild, unknown frontier. Emperor Meiji himself was added in 1964 as the fourth deity.

由緒 · Why It Matters
明治維新が生んだ北海道守護の神。
Unlike most ichinomiya with ancient roots, Hokkaido Jingu was deliberately created in 1869 — the year the Meiji government began the systematic settlement of Japan's wild northern island. It is the only ichinomiya founded as a national development project, its deities chosen by imperial decree to bless an entire frontier.
A shrine built for an impossible mission. When the Meiji government sent settlers north into Hokkaido, they faced bitter winters, untamed wilderness, and a land that had never been farmed at scale. The emperor enshrined the three gods in Tokyo first, then ceremonially moved them to Sapporo — planting a sacred seed in the heart of the new city. Today, the shrine's 180,000 sqm of old-growth forest feels ancient, but it is entirely the product of deliberate planting and sacred care over 150 years.

2 Deep Insight · 深掘り リピーターのこだわり — 知る人ぞ知る 深度探索 · 行家才知道的
Spring
2
~1,400 cherry trees — Sapporo's finest hanami spot
Because Hokkaido blooms a full month after Honshu (late April to mid-May), this is your last chance for cherry blossoms if you missed them elsewhere. Around 50 food stalls line the paths during peak bloom. Also ~250 plum trees open alongside — a rare double bloom.
Nature
Wild Ezo squirrels & rare birds in a city forest
2
The 180,000 sqm of old-growth shrine forest hosts Ezo red squirrels (エゾリス), woodpeckers, and dozens of migratory bird species. Birders bring binoculars in spring and autumn. Spotting a squirrel on the approach path is genuinely common.
Festival
Hokkaido Jingu Sai (札幌まつり) — June festival
Every June, ~1,200 participants dress in Heian-period court costumes for a grand procession through central Sapporo. Called the "Sapporo Festival," it is Hokkaido's signal that summer has arrived. The mikoshi procession is elaborate and photograph-friendly.
Unique
A 六花亭 (Rokkatei) sweets shop — inside the shrine grounds
Hokkaido's most beloved confectionery brand has a branch inside the shrine precincts. After praying, buy fresh-baked butter-and-bean-paste manjū (butter sandwiches are their signature). A second sweet shop, Jingu Chaya, sells shrine-branded treats beside it.
History
Rebuilt from ashes — twice as meaningful
The original shrine buildings were destroyed by arson fire in 1974. The current structures — rebuilt in 1978 in traditional Nagare-zukuri style — are authentically faithful to the lost originals. The rebuilding itself became a symbol of Hokkaido's resilient spirit.

2 Eat · Stay · Explore 周辺の滞在 — 食・泊・次の旅へ 周边吃住行 · 延伸旅程
Inside the Shrine 🍡
六花亭 Rokkatei Shrine Branch
Hokkaido's iconic confectionery shop has a rare in-shrine location. Their satsu no mori mochi and fresh butter sandwiches are the ultimate post-prayer treat. Sells out early on busy days — go straight there after praying.
Local Ramen 🍜
Sapporo Miso Ramen
The Maruyama /円山 neighborhood is full of excellent ramen shops. Sapporo's signature is miso-based with corn and butter — rich and warming after a cold morning visit. Try Ramen Yokocho downtown (20 min by subway) for the classic experience.
Stay Nearby 🏨
Susukino / Odori Hotels
Central Sapporo (Susukino, Odori) has hundreds of hotels from budget to luxury — all within 20 min by subway. JR Tower Hotel Nikko Sapporo has panoramic city views from the top of Sapporo Station. Book early for cherry blossom season.
Adjacent Sight 🦁
Maruyama Zoo · 5 min walk
One of Japan's best zoos is a 5-minute walk from the shrine — famous for its polar bears, snow leopards, and Hokkaido brown bears. Combine shrine + zoo for a full Maruyama day, especially perfect for families.
Day Trip 🌾
Furano & Biei Flower Fields
In July-August, lavender fields of Furano (2 hrs by JR) turn Hokkaido purple. Biei's rolling patchwork hills are iconic. These are among Japan's most Instagrammed landscapes — easily reached by rental car or tourist bus from Sapporo.
Winter Bonus ⛄
Sapporo Snow Festival (Feb)
Every February, the Snow Festival fills Odori Park with massive ice sculptures. Combine a shrine hatsumode visit (New Year) or Snow Festival trip to see Hokkaido Jingu in its most dramatic state — deep snow, silent forest, lantern light.

The creation of Hokkaido Jingu in 1869 was unlike any other shrine founding in Japanese history. Rather than honoring a local legend or an ancient land deity, it was a deliberate act of state — Emperor Meiji chose three gods whose combined powers exactly matched what Hokkaido's settlers would need: land protection, agricultural development, and medical/technical knowledge.

This made Hokkaido Jingu a "purpose-built" shrine for a national mission. The Meiji government was simultaneously sending thousands of settlers, soldiers, and engineers north to transform Hokkaido from Ainu homeland and wilderness into agricultural land. The shrine served as their spiritual anchor in an otherwise deeply unfamiliar world.

Today, the shrine's 180,000 sqm of forest in the middle of Sapporo — a city of 1.9 million — is itself a miracle of planning. The old trees that shade the approach path were planted by hand in the 1870s and 1880s. What feels timeless was once bare ground. This is the real frontier miracle: not conquest, but cultivation.
